# Criterion D6: Market Regime Adaptability

## Definition

Does the report's strategy adapt to the diagnosed market regime (bull, bear, stagflation, deflation, regime transition)? A strategy that ignores macro context is fragile to regime change.

## Behavioral anchors (from BARS pilot v1)

- **Level 2 (poor):** Strategy ignoring regime (e.g., classic 60/40 allocation recommended for explicit stagflation scenario; growth assumptions used in recession analysis).
- **Level 5 (average):** Regime mentioned but strategy not adapted accordingly.
- **Level 8 (good):** Strategy adapted to regime with explicit rationale. Observed in R1 (e.g., S2-R1-G line 23: `macroeconomic confluence introduces a severe, dual-front crisis: erosion of real asset returns... and asymmetric expansion of inflation-linked long-term liabilities` — regime diagnosis + adapted strategy (LDI redesign)).
- **Level 10 (excellent):** Plus variants for different regimes and transition triggers between them. Aspirational.

## Scoring method

**Type:** regime alignment check.

**Procedure:**

1. **Regime diagnosis identification.** Does the report identify the current/projected market regime?
   - Bull / bear / sideways
   - Inflation regime (normal / disinflation / stagflation / hyperinflation)
   - Interest rate regime (zero / normal / restrictive)
   - Growth regime (recession / slow / strong)
2. **Strategy-regime alignment.** Does the recommended strategy match the diagnosed regime?
   - For stagflation (S2): expect inflation hedges, real assets, shorter duration
   - For recession (any): expect quality bias, downside protection, liquidity preservation
   - For tax reform in low-growth (S1): expect attention to behavioral response
   - For UBI in high-debt regime (S3): expect fiscal-first framing
3. **Transition triggers.** Does the report specify what would change the regime diagnosis or trigger strategy shifts?
4. **Per-case relevance.** Note: regime relevance varies by case:
   - S1 (tax reform): medium relevance — fiscal regime matters but not central
   - S2 (pension allocation under stagflation): HIGH relevance — entire question is about regime
   - S3 (UBI Italy): medium relevance — fiscal/demographic regime central

Score guidance:
- Diagnosis + adapted strategy + regime variants + triggers → 9–10
- Diagnosis + adapted strategy + explicit rationale → 7–8
- Diagnosis present, strategy partially adapted → 5–6
- Diagnosis mentioned, no adaptation → 3–4
- Regime ignored → 0–2

## Asymmetry per response type

- Apply uniformly. For case S2 (where regime is explicitly given in prompt), expect all response types to engage with it.
- Note per-case sub-weight informally: D6 for S1/S3 may be less determinative than for S2.
