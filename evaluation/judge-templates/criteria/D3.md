# Criterion D3: Risk & Asymmetry Resilience

## Definition

Does the report assess the risk profile of recommendations, including cost of capital, asymmetric return distribution, and tail risks ("Black Swans")? Sound financial analysis requires explicit risk quantification — promised returns are worthless without exposure quantification.

## Behavioral anchors (from BARS pilot v1)

- **Level 2 (poor):** Risk ignored; "guaranteed returns" or recommendations with no downside discussion.
- **Level 5 (average):** Risk mentioned but not quantified (e.g., "inflation may rise further").
- **Level 8 (good):** Risk-reward ratio explicit + sensitivity analysis. Observed in R1 (e.g., S2-R1-C line 47: `commodity hedging strength is driven mainly by energy inflation rather than broad core inflation` — explicit risk decomposition + sensitivity (energy vs core)).
- **Level 10 (excellent):** Plus stress test of extreme scenarios with quantified exposure. Observed in R1 (e.g., S2-R1-G lines 47–48: `fund... must structurally and permanently increase their unencumbered cash and highly liquid collateral buffers to survive standard deviation rate shocks` — explicit reference to 2022 UK gilt crisis as stress test).

## Scoring method

**Type:** risk metrics check.

**Procedure:**

1. **Risk metric inventory.** Look for explicit risk measures in the report:
   - **Quantitative:** VaR (Value at Risk), CVaR / Expected Shortfall, Sharpe ratio, Sortino ratio, beta, max drawdown, volatility (σ), tracking error
   - **Qualitative:** explicit risk scenarios, stress tests, "what if" analysis
   - **Asymmetry:** discussion of skewness, kurtosis, fat tails, downside vs upside
2. **Risk decomposition.** Does the report decompose risks by source (e.g., for S2: interest rate risk vs credit risk vs inflation risk; for S1: legal risk vs economic risk vs political risk)?
3. **Cost of capital / opportunity cost.** Does the report consider the cost of capital or opportunity cost of recommended actions?
4. **Tail/extreme scenarios.** Does the report discuss what happens in tail scenarios (recession, crisis, regime change)?
5. **Quantified exposure.** When risks are identified, are exposures quantified (e.g., "if inflation rises 1pp, NPV decreases by X%")?

Score guidance:
- Multiple quantitative metrics + decomposition + tail scenarios with quantified exposure → 9–10
- Quantitative metrics + decomposition + qualitative tail discussion → 7–8
- Risk identified and partly quantified → 5–6
- Risk mentioned but not quantified → 3–4
- Risk ignored → 0–2

## Per-case context

- **S1** (turnover tax): risks include legal challenge risk (CJEU rulings), economic distortion risk (sectoral collapse), political risk (reform failure)
- **S2** (pension allocation): risks include interest rate risk, inflation risk, credit risk, liquidity risk (e.g., 2022 UK gilt crisis), funding ratio risk
- **S3** (Italy UBI): risks include fiscal sustainability risk, sovereign spread risk, labor supply risk, political backlash risk

## Asymmetry per response type

- **AKM:** the Critic role often raises risk concerns. Expect engagement with risks.
- **B1:** depends on base model.
- **R1:** Deep Research training often emphasizes risk frames. High baseline.
