# Criterion B6: Solution Novelty

## Definition

Does the report go beyond textbook recommendations? Does it offer expert insight — a non-obvious framing, a novel synthesis of two areas of knowledge, or a counterintuitive but well-grounded recommendation?

This is the highest-bar criterion in dimension B. Routine analysis can score well on B1–B5 (multi-dim decomposition, causal chains, second-order thinking) but fail B6 if the conclusions are predictable from the literature.

## Subweight = 0.5 (critical note)

This criterion has a sub-weight of **0.5** within dimension B (vs. 1.0 default). Rationale: there is an inherent asymmetry between AKM (open-weights, limited recency in training data) and R1 (Deep Research with access to current literature). R1 can systematically score higher on novelty simply by accessing recent papers AKM cannot reach. To prevent this from dominating the dimension, B6 is weighted at half. The post-processing script applies this sub-weight when computing dimension B mean.

**You do NOT need to apply the sub-weight yourself.** Score B6 honestly (0–10); the aggregation handles weighting.

## Behavioral anchors (from BARS pilot v1)

- **Level 2 (poor):** Textbook recommendations ("consider alternatives", "requires further analysis"). No specific insights.
- **Level 5 (average):** Sound but predictable recommendations aligned with mainstream literature. Observed in R1 (e.g., S2-R1-G recommends "25% inflation-linked sovereign bonds, 15% short-duration credit, 25% equities tilted value/quality" — solid but well-known LDI playbook).
- **Level 8 (good):** At least one non-standard insight that goes beyond current literature, or a reframing of the problem that opens new angles. Observed in R1 (e.g., S1-R1-C line 45: `either the base is truly universal and the rate is around 3.6%, or the rate climbs into a range that becomes even harder to absorb commercially` — "brutal arithmetic" framing presents the trade-off more operationally than typical literature).
- **Level 10 (excellent):** Synthesis of ≥2 knowledge areas in a novel way; recommendation that cannot be found in any single source paper.

## Scoring method

**Type:** baseline comparison + qualitative judgment.

### Per-case baseline novelty (standard recommendations to compare against)

**S1 (PL turnover tax):**
- Implement broad turnover tax to replace PIT+CIT
- Restrict to specific sectors (retail tax, financial tax)
- Reject turnover tax entirely; reform existing system
- Mixed regime: turnover for small businesses, profit-based for large

**S2 (Pension fund allocation under inflation):**
- Tilt toward inflation-linked bonds (TIPS, linkers)
- Reduce duration of nominal bonds
- Tilt equities toward value/quality/dividend stocks
- Add commodities for inflation hedge
- Add real assets (infrastructure, real estate with CPI escalators)

**S3 (Italy UBI):**
- Implement debt-financed full UBI
- Implement revenue-neutral partial UBI
- Reject UBI; reform existing AdI/SFL welfare schemes
- Mixed: targeted UBI for specific groups (youth, women, unemployed)

### Procedure

1. Identify the report's main recommendations.
2. Map them onto the baseline list above. How many recommendations exceed the baseline (provide non-obvious framing, novel parameter, or unconventional synthesis)?
3. Look for novel framing of the problem (not just novel solutions). Examples: "brutal arithmetic" framing in S1-R1-C; "regime break" framing in S2-R1-C.
4. Check for inter-disciplinary synthesis: does the report integrate insights from finance + behavioral economics + political economy + history?

Score guidance:
- ≥2 novel insights + cross-disciplinary synthesis → 8–10
- 1 novel insight or framing → 6–7
- All recommendations on baseline list, but well-argued → 5
- Recommendations on baseline list, weakly argued → 3–4
- Trivial / boilerplate recommendations → 0–2

## R1 advantage caveat

Be aware that R1 (Deep Research) has access to current literature that AKM and B1 may not. A novel insight from R1 may simply reflect access to a paper from this month. Do not penalize AKM for the literature gap; do not over-credit R1 for it. The 0.5 sub-weight is the structural correction.

## Asymmetry per response type

- **AKM:** open-weights models with knowledge cutoff. May produce novel framings via the Critic-Analyst dialectic. Hypothesis: AKM > B1 on B6 because Critic forces non-obvious angles.
- **B1:** single-shot; novelty unlikely to emerge without explicit prompt push.
- **R1:** access to current papers → systematic novelty advantage (mitigated by 0.5 sub-weight).
