# Criterion A5: Common Sense Robustness

## Definition

Does the report avoid economic and mathematical absurdities? Are statements consistent with basic economic logic? When the report makes counter-intuitive claims, are they explicitly explained and grounded?

This criterion captures a class of failures different from factual inaccuracy: structural absurdities that any economist would catch on first read. Examples: negative interest rates accompanying hyperinflation without explanation; net margins above 100%; GDP growth above 20% YoY for a developed economy; total tax revenue exceeding total GDP; etc.

## Behavioral anchors (from BARS pilot v1)

- **Level 2 (poor):** Visible economic absurdities present and uncorrected. E.g., "the company has a 110% net margin", "interest rates are -5% to combat 8% inflation" without context.
- **Level 5 (average):** Occasional questionable statements (e.g., suspicious round numbers presented without estimation context, ratios that seem implausible).
- **Level 8 (good):** All statements economically sensible; counter-intuitive claims are accompanied by explicit explanation. Observed in R1 (e.g., S1-R1-C line 49: `at 3.6%... burden equals 72% of profit when margin is 5%, 120% when 3%, 180% when 2%` — the >100% figure is mathematically correct and immediately contextualized).
- **Level 10 (excellent):** Plus active identification and explanation of counter-intuitive phenomena: "this seems counterintuitive because..." or "while X looks surprising, it reflects Y". Observed in R1 (e.g., S2-R1-C line 35 contrasts "higher nominal yields" with "persistent inflation surprise" — explains a non-obvious distinction).

## Scoring method

**Type:** red flag check + qualitative review.

**Procedure:**

1. **Red flag scan.** Look for the following classes of absurdity:
   - **Sign violations:** negative values where impossible (e.g., negative GDP for a positive economy, negative revenues without write-off context).
   - **Ratio violations:** percentages above 100% where economically impossible (e.g., net margin > 100%, default rate > 100% on a portfolio).
   - **Economic law violations:** statements contradicting basic principles (Walras' law, money-market arbitrage, supply-demand balance) without explicit context.
   - **Magnitude violations:** order-of-magnitude errors (e.g., country GDP confused with city GDP; trillions confused with millions; bps confused with %).
   - **Time-frame violations:** annual vs quarterly vs monthly figures conflated.
2. **Context check.** When a counter-intuitive number appears, is it framed and explained? A 180% figure can be perfectly correct (as in R1 S1-C line 49) if the calculation is shown.
3. **Domain-specific sanity.** Per case context:
   - **S1 (PL tax):** rates between 0–60%, GDP shares 0–100%, currency PLN
   - **S2 (pension):** allocations sum to 100%, real returns -10% to +10% typical, durations 0–30 years
   - **S3 (UBI Italy):** debt/GDP 100–200% range, employment rates 40–80%, transfer amounts in EUR

Score guidance:
- No absurdities + counter-intuitive claims well-explained → 8–10
- Minor questionable statements → 5–7
- Several questionable numbers → 3–5
- Clear absurdities → 0–3

## Asymmetry per response type

- Apply uniformly. All three response types should pass basic economic sanity checks. R1 (Deep Research) had no absurdities in pilot; expect similar for high-quality AKM.
