# Criterion E3: Tone Consistency

## Definition

Maintenance of professional, cool, expert tone throughout. The style of communication builds (or destroys) institutional trust.

E3 differs from C3 (Objectivity & Sycophancy Mitigation):
- **C3** assesses substance: does the report avoid pleasing the user with positive framing when negative is warranted?
- **E3** assesses tone: is the writing style professionally consistent, neither too casual nor over-academic?

## Behavioral anchors (from BARS pilot v1)

- **Level 2 (poor):** Marketing style or colloquial (exclamation marks, slang, informal idioms).
- **Level 5 (average):** Mixed; some sections professional, others too casual or too academic.
- **Level 8 (good):** Consistently expert tone throughout. Observed in R1 (all R1 reports — cool, analytical, neutral tone).
- **Level 10 (excellent):** Plus terminology consistency (each term defined at first use, then used consistently). Observed in R1 (e.g., S2-R1-G defines `Liability-Driven Investing (LDI)` at first use, then uses consistently).

## Scoring method

**Type:** tone marker check.

**Procedure:**

1. **Anti-marker scan.** Look for tone-breaking elements:
   - Exclamation marks (in body text — headers OK)
   - Slang: "kick the can", "low-hanging fruit", "no-brainer"
   - Informal idioms: "at the end of the day", "all things considered" (excessive use)
   - Overly casual phrases: "let's", "you'll see", "we're"
   - Marketing buzzwords: "game-changing", "disruptive", "innovative" (without quantitative backing)
   - Hedge filler: "essentially", "basically", "fundamentally" used repetitively
2. **Pro-marker scan.** Look for elements of expert tone:
   - Technical precision: specific terms used correctly
   - Measured language: "indicates", "suggests", "supports", "argues for"
   - Neutral stance: avoiding emotional vocabulary
   - Defined terminology: first-use definitions followed by consistent usage
3. **Section-by-section consistency.** Compare tone in introduction, middle, conclusion. Are they stylistically aligned?

Score guidance:
- Consistent expert tone + defined terminology → 9–10
- Consistent expert tone → 7–8
- Mostly expert, occasional informality → 5–6
- Inconsistent tone → 3–4
- Casual or marketing style → 0–2

## Asymmetry per response type

- Apply uniformly. All response types should produce expert-grade tone for institutional financial analysis.
