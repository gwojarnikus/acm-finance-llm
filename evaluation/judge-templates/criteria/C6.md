# Criterion C6: Dialectical Resolution Quality

## Definition

Does the report *resolve* identified contradictions and tensions, or merely list them? Quality of argumentation *for* the chosen position against named alternatives.

C6 complements C2 (Multiperspectival Synthesis):
- C2 measures the *presence* of multiple perspectives (divergent thinking).
- C6 measures the *quality of resolution* between them (convergent synthesis).

This distinction draws on multi-agent debate literature (Du, Li, Torralba, Tenenbaum, & Mordatch, 2023; Liang et al., 2023): a system can be good at *generating* divergent views but poor at *converging* on the best one — or vice versa.

## Behavioral anchors (from BARS pilot v1)

- **Level 2 (poor):** Contradictions ignored or falsely unified ("both sides have a point" without engagement).
- **Level 5 (average):** Contradictions listed but no resolution ("this remains debated", "scholars disagree").
- **Level 8 (good):** Contradictions named + justified preferred resolution. Observed in R1 (e.g., S3-R1-C line 65: `the prudent route is not a national full UBI, but a strictly debt-neutral, limited-scope quasi-universal transfer with automatic review clauses` — resolution of UBI vs targeted welfare tension via *via media*).
- **Level 10 (excellent):** Plus reference to an explicit decision criterion (utilitarian / Pareto / deontological / fiduciary) + sensitivity analysis on the criterion. Observed in R1 (e.g., S2-R1-C line 35: explicit criterion "real funded status" as primary measure resolves between "higher nominal yields" and "inflation hedging" trade-off).

## Scoring method

**Type:** contradiction-resolution check.

**Procedure:**

1. **Contradiction inventory.** Identify the tensions or contradictions surfaced in the report. Common types:
   - Short-term vs long-term outcomes
   - Growth vs stability
   - Efficiency vs equity
   - Individual vs collective interest
   - Different stakeholder perspectives
2. **Resolution check.** For each contradiction, does the report propose a resolution? Possible resolutions include:
   - Choosing one side with justification ("we prefer X because Y")
   - Synthesizing both ("X and Y are compatible if Z")
   - Identifying conditions when each holds ("X in scenario A, Y in scenario B")
   - Rejecting the dichotomy ("the apparent trade-off is illusory because...")
3. **Argumentation quality.** Is the resolution argued or merely asserted? Strong resolution has:
   - Explicit decision criterion ("we resolve in favor of X because criterion C is more important")
   - Evidence backing the criterion choice
   - Acknowledgment of what the resolution loses
4. **Sensitivity check.** If the decision criterion is contested, does the report discuss what happens under alternative criteria?

Score guidance:
- All contradictions resolved + explicit criterion + sensitivity → 9–10
- All contradictions resolved + justified preference → 7–8
- Contradictions named with partial resolution → 5–6
- Contradictions named without resolution → 3–4
- Contradictions ignored or falsely unified → 0–2

## Asymmetry per response type

- **AKM:** the Moderator role should drive resolution after Critic surfaces contradictions. Hypothesis: AKM scores higher on the C2 + C6 combination than B1 (divergence by Critic, convergence by Moderator).
- **B1:** single-shot; resolution depends on base model.
- **R1:** Deep Research training tends to produce well-resolved narratives. High baseline.
