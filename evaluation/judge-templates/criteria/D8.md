# Criterion D8: Report Coherence

## Definition

Does the report read as the work of a single author, despite the underlying complexity of the production process (multi-agent, multi-iteration, multi-source)? The final product for a user should be coherent — hiding the internal complexity.

For AKM specifically, this is a strong test: the report is produced by Analyst-Critic-Moderator iterating over multiple steps, with potentially conflicting suggestions integrated. A high D8 score means the Moderator successfully integrated everything into a unified voice.

## Behavioral anchors (from BARS pilot v1)

- **Level 2 (poor):** Visible "seams" — phrases like "the Critic noted that...", "as discussed earlier but reconsidering...", different voice in different sections.
- **Level 5 (average):** Style inconsistencies between sections (technical section next to marketing section).
- **Level 8 (good):** Consistent style and voice throughout. Observed in R1 (all 6 R1 reports show consistent expert tone and terminology from beginning to end).
- **Level 10 (excellent):** Plus narrative arc (problem → analysis → resolution). Observed in R1 (e.g., S3-R1-C: explicit arc — Problem (high-debt Italy + UBI tension) → Analysis (fiscal/demographic/labour) → Synthesis (UBI structurally misaligned) → Recommendation (targeted welfare instead)).

## Scoring method

**Type:** coherence assessment.

**Procedure:**

1. **Voice consistency check.** Read the introduction, a middle section, and the conclusion. Are they written in the same voice (formal/informal, technical/accessible, analytical/persuasive)?
2. **Style consistency.** Are sentence structures consistent? Vocabulary level? Use of jargon?
3. **Seam detection.** Look for "seams" indicating multi-agent or multi-iteration origin:
   - Explicit references to the production process ("the analysis above", "as we noted earlier")
   - Style breaks between sections
   - Repeated argumentation in different wording (suggests reconciliation of independent drafts)
   - Sudden shifts in confidence level
4. **Narrative arc check.** Does the report tell a coherent story (problem → analysis → resolution)? Or is it a collection of related observations?
5. **Terminology consistency.** Are technical terms used consistently (same term for same concept; defined once and used throughout)?

Score guidance:
- Consistent voice + style + terminology + narrative arc → 9–10
- Consistent voice + style → 7–8
- Mostly consistent, minor inconsistencies → 5–6
- Visible inconsistencies between sections → 3–4
- Obvious seams or fragmented → 0–2

## Research hypothesis

A good AKM has BOTH:
- High C1 (Counter-Argumentation — Critic's effect visible in content)
- High D8 (Report Coherence — Moderator's effect on voice)

A high C1 + low D8 = the multi-agent process is visible (Critic raised concerns but Moderator didn't smooth them).
A low C1 + high D8 = the Critic role was weak (single coherent voice without critical pressure).
The hypothesized AKM advantage is the simultaneous achievement of both.

## Asymmetry per response type

- **AKM:** D8 is hardest test — requires Moderator to successfully integrate multi-agent input.
- **B1:** single-shot, single voice — should naturally be coherent (unless model itself has style breaks).
- **R1:** Deep Research training favors coherent narrative arcs. High baseline.
