# Criterion A4: Temporal Awareness

## Definition

Does the report correctly identify the temporal relationship between the model's training cutoff, the current date, and the dates of cited data? Does it avoid anachronisms (citing "recent data" from years ago without flagging staleness; referencing events after model's likely cutoff)?

In financial analysis, temporal precision matters enormously: yesterday's interest rate is not last year's, a 2024 forecast is not a 2026 result, "current" CPI must be dated. Temporal sloppiness undermines the credibility of every conclusion.

## Behavioral anchors (from BARS pilot v1)

- **Level 2 (poor):** Anachronisms present: citing "recent data" from before model cutoff without warning; no dates on cited facts; references to events from the future or distant past as "current." Hypothetical for R1.
- **Level 5 (average):** Dates present on most citations, but no explicit reflection on cutoff or data vintage. "Today is..." not anchored to specific date.
- **Level 8 (good):** Every numerical fact has a date; report uses "as of [date]" language; no anachronisms detected. Observed in R1 (e.g., S2-R1-C line 25: `U.S. 10-year Treasury yield on 7 May 2026: 4.41%` — date specified per data point).
- **Level 10 (excellent):** Plus explicit warning about cutoff implications for projections; reasoning about how data freshness affects conclusions ("this estimate is based on Q1 2026 data and may need revision if Q2 prints differ materially").

## Scoring method

**Type:** static analysis with date enumeration.

**Procedure:**

1. **Date inventory.** For each numerical fact or empirical claim, check whether a date is specified (either inline or via the citation).
   - Examples of dated facts: "PIT revenue PLN 202.8 billion (Ministry of Finance, 2026)" ✓; "advanced economy GDP growth 1.8% in 2026 (IMF, 2026)" ✓
   - Examples of undated facts: "PIT revenue is approximately X billion" ✗; "the inflation rate is currently elevated" ✗
2. **Plausibility check.** Are the dates plausible — not from the future (unless explicitly projection), not from a decade ago as "current"?
3. **Cutoff awareness.** Does the report reflect on model knowledge cutoff? Look for phrases like "as of [date]", "data available through [date]", "subject to revision when [later data] is published", or explicit "Today is YYYY-MM-DD" anchoring.
4. **"As of" usage.** Does the report use "as of [date]" or equivalent for time-sensitive data points?

Score guidance:
- All dates present + cutoff awareness + "as of" usage → 8–10
- Most dates present + some temporal anchoring → 5–7
- Dates present but no temporal reflection → 3–5
- Anachronisms or missing dates → 0–3

## Asymmetry per response type

- **AKM:** prompt mandates "Today is {{$now}}" header in `akm-prompts-en.md` — Analyst is forced to anchor temporally. Check whether body of report reflects this anchoring.
- **B1:** less structural pressure; assess what's present.
- **R1:** Deep Research has live web search access; dates should be precise and current. Expect higher A4 baseline.
