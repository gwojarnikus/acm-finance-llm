# Criterion C2: Multiperspectival Synthesis

## Definition

Does the report integrate conflicting points of view (Profit vs Risk, Short-term vs Long-term, Individual vs Social, Efficiency vs Equity)? A flat, single-perspective analysis is rarely adequate for financial-economic policy where stakeholders have divergent interests.

C2 differs from B1 (Multi-dimensional Decomposition): B1 asks "does the report look at the problem from multiple angles?" (e.g., legal, economic, technical); C2 asks "does the report integrate genuinely conflicting perspectives within those angles?" (e.g., labor vs capital interests within the economic angle).

## Distinction from C6 (Dialectical Resolution Quality)

C2 measures the *presence* of multiple perspectives. C6 measures the *quality of resolution* between them. A report can have high C2 (many perspectives present) but low C6 (no synthesis) or vice versa.

## Behavioral anchors (from BARS pilot v1)

- **Level 2 (poor):** Flat perspective (only optimistic, or only pessimistic, or only one stakeholder's viewpoint).
- **Level 5 (average):** 2 perspectives mentioned but not integrated (e.g., economic angle next to legal, without interaction).
- **Level 8 (good):** 3+ perspectives with attempted synthesis. Observed in R1 (e.g., S1-R1-C integrates arithmetic (3.6%) + economic (sectoral impact) + legal (EU law) into a unified thesis).
- **Level 10 (excellent):** Hegelian synthesis — thesis and antithesis yielding a synthesis that reveals complexity. Observed in R1 (e.g., S3-R1-C line 37: `In Italy, a fiscally neutral UBI is likely to be too small to be transformative, while a transformative UBI is too large to be fiscally neutral` — explicit antinomy with trade-off synthesis).

## Scoring method

**Type:** perspective identification + integration assessment.

**Procedure:**

1. **Perspective inventory.** Identify distinct perspectives represented in the report. Categories to look for:
   - Stakeholder perspectives: labor vs capital, consumers vs producers, taxpayers vs beneficiaries
   - Temporal: short-term vs long-term
   - Spatial: regional/local vs national vs international
   - Domain: economic vs legal vs political vs social
   - Risk frames: downside vs upside, expected vs tail
2. **Integration check.** Are perspectives juxtaposed (just listed in different sections) or genuinely integrated (one perspective informs the analysis of another)?
3. **Tension surfacing.** Does the report explicitly surface tensions between perspectives ("this benefits group X but harms group Y")?
4. **Synthesis test.** Is there a synthesis that goes beyond "both sides have a point" — e.g., a reframing that resolves the tension?

Score guidance:
- ≥3 perspectives integrated + explicit synthesis → 9–10
- 3 perspectives with attempted synthesis → 7–8
- 2–3 perspectives, juxtaposed → 5–6
- 2 perspectives mentioned → 3–4
- Single perspective → 0–2

## Asymmetry per response type

- **AKM:** the Critic agent should surface alternative perspectives. Expect higher C2.
- **B1:** dependent on base model's tendency to consider multiple stakeholders.
- **R1:** Deep Research training favors multi-perspective synthesis. High baseline.
