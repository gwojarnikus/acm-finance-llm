# Criterion D2: Regulatory Compliance

## Definition

Does the report comply with — and correctly cite — the regulatory framework relevant to its case? Financial-economic analysis must operate within legal constraints. Recommendations that violate regulations create legal risk exceeding any potential benefit.

## Pre-requisite: per-case briefing

This criterion requires the per-case briefing document (D2 briefing) that lists the key regulatory references for the case. If the briefing is not provided in the prompt, set `score: null` and `skipped_reason: "per-case D2 briefing not provided"`.

**Per-case briefing content** (loaded into `{briefing_D2}` placeholder):

For S1 (PL turnover tax):
- VAT Directive 2006/112/EC, especially Article 401 (turnover tax prohibition); essential characteristics test
- TFEU: Article 107 (state aid), Article 110 (internal taxation), Article 30 (border formalities)
- Directive (EU) 2022/2523 (Pillar Two minimum tax)
- CJEU jurisprudence: Case C-475/17 (IRAP precedent), Case C-562/19 P (Polish retail tax), Case C-323/18 P (Hungarian advertising tax)

For S2 (pension fund allocation):
- IORP II Directive (EU pension regulation)
- Solvency II (insurance, applies to some pension structures)
- Polish-specific: OFE, PPK, PPE rules (if Polish pension)
- EBA guidelines on investment policies

For S3 (Italy UBI):
- TUE Article 153 (EU social policy competence)
- Italian welfare framework: AdI (Assegno di Inclusione), SFL (Supporto per la Formazione e il Lavoro)
- ESPN (European Social Policy Network) Italy reports
- EU Stability and Growth Pact (relevant for debt-financed UBI)

## Behavioral anchors (from BARS pilot v1)

- **Level 2 (poor):** Regulation violations or complete ignorance of regulatory framework.
- **Level 5 (average):** Awareness of regulations (mentions "EU rules" or "tax law"), no specific paragraphs cited.
- **Level 8 (good):** Specific paragraphs/directives cited correctly. Observed in R1 (e.g., S1-R1-C line 33: `Article 401 of the VAT Directive`, `Directive (EU) 2022/2523`, `Case C-475/17`, `TFEU` — precise per-case references).
- **Level 10 (excellent):** Plus analysis of interactions between regulations. Observed in R1 (e.g., S1-R1-C line 71: analysis of Pillar Two (Dir. 2022/2523) interaction with hypothetical turnover tax — shows `covered taxes are fundamentally income taxes, not non-income-based levies` forces retention of CIT layer).

## Scoring method

**Type:** per-case briefing cross-check + contextual correctness.

**Procedure:**

1. **Citation match.** From the per-case briefing list, how many of the expected regulations are cited in the report? Count.
2. **Citation accuracy.** Are the citations correct? Check:
   - Is the paragraph/article number correct? (e.g., "Article 401 of VAT Directive" — correct; "Article 102" — incorrect)
   - Is the directive number correct? (e.g., "2006/112/EC" — correct VAT Directive)
   - Is the case citation correct? (e.g., "Case C-475/17" — correct IRAP)
3. **Contextual application.** Are the regulations applied correctly to the case?
   - Does the report understand what the regulation actually requires?
   - Does it apply the regulation to the right scenario?
4. **Interaction analysis.** Does the report consider interactions between multiple regulations (e.g., VAT Directive + State Aid + Pillar Two)?

Score guidance:
- ≥4 expected regulations cited correctly + contextual application + interaction analysis → 9–10
- ≥3 expected regulations cited correctly + contextual application → 7–8
- 2 regulations correctly cited and applied → 5–6
- 1 regulation cited, applied with minor errors → 3–4
- No regulations cited or violations present → 0–2

## Asymmetry per response type

- **R1:** Deep Research has access to EUR-Lex, CJEU databases — expect high D2 baseline.
- **AKM/B1:** depend on whether base model has internalized regulatory knowledge. Use briefing as ground truth, not the model's outputs.
