# Criterion A1: Data Grounding Precision

## Definition

Does every numerical fact or expository claim carry a bibliographic citation in APA 7 format with a verifiable date and author? Assess the ratio of grounded facts to total facts.

A "grounded fact" is a discrete numerical value, named statistic, or substantive empirical claim that is accompanied either by an inline citation (e.g., `(Ministry of Finance, 2026)`, `(IMF, 2025)`) or by a clearly linked entry in a Sources/References section. A "fact" without grounding is a quantitative or factual claim that appears without traceable provenance.

## Behavioral anchors (from BARS pilot v1, calibrated on R1 sample)

- **Level 0:** Not used for A1 (level 2 is the lowest meaningful anchor).
- **Level 2 (poor):** Only 2–3 citations across the entire report; most numerical claims lack attribution. No Sources section. Hypothetical anchor — not observed in R1; typical of weak B1 outputs.
- **Level 5 (average):** Approximately 50% of facts are grounded; some citations are generic (e.g., "KPMG, 2025" with no specific report title). Sources section incomplete or inconsistent.
- **Level 8 (good):** Every numerical fact has a precise APA 7 reference with author, date, and title. At most 1–2 minor claims without explicit citation. Observed in R1 (e.g., S1-R1-C line 11: `PLN 594.6 billion total revenues... Source (APA 7): Ministry of Finance. (2026, February 24). Szacunkowe wykonanie budżetu państwa w 2025 r. [Estimated execution of the state budget in 2025]. Government of Poland.`).
- **Level 10 (excellent):** Full grounding + APA 7 compliance + consistent access dates + cross-references between citations + identification of source conflicts where they exist. Aspirational; rarely observed in full.

## Scoring method

**Type:** static analysis with explicit count.

**Procedure:**

1. Identify and count `total_facts` — discrete numerical or substantive empirical claims in the report. Examples of a fact: "PLN 294.6 billion total PIT+CIT", "Italy's debt at 137% of GDP", "Federal funds rate 3.64% in April 2026". Do NOT count generic descriptive statements like "tax revenue is significant" as facts.
2. Identify and count `grounded_facts` — facts that are accompanied by an inline citation or are linked to a Sources/References entry. The citation must contain author/organization + year at minimum.
3. Compute: `raw_score = 10 × (grounded_facts / total_facts)`.
4. Apply quality adjustments:
   - If APA 7 format is consistently used in citations: no change.
   - If citations are present but format is sloppy (missing dates, no author, broken URLs in source list): subtract 1 from raw_score.
   - If Sources section is missing entirely (even when inline citations exist): subtract 1.
5. Round to nearest 0.5 increment. Final score in `[0, 10]`.

## Asymmetry per response type

- **AKM:** prompt mandates a dedicated `**Sources:**` section in APA 7 (per `prompts/agents/akm-prompts-en.md`). Expect this format; absence is a strong signal.
- **B1:** prompt does not mandate a separate Sources section; citations may appear inline in body text. Do not penalize for missing Sources section — count grounded facts the same way.
- **R1:** uses inline citations such as `citeturn29view0` (R1-C) or numbered footnotes with References at end (R1-G). Count both formats as grounded; normalize format differences in your assessment.

## R1 self-reported metadata handling

R1 reports may include `sources_count_reported` in a metadata footer (e.g., `sources_consulted_estimated: 24`). **IGNORE this value.** Count grounded facts directly from the report body.

## Soft Veto trigger

If your score is below 3.0, the post-processing script will activate λ = 0.5 (Soft Veto). Do not adjust your score to avoid triggering veto — score what you observe.
